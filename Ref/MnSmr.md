## manu
#### 1.1
मनुमेकाग्रमासीनमभिगम्य महर्षयः ।

The Great Sages, having approached Manu, seated with mind calm and collected, 

प्रतिपूज्य यथान्यायमिदं वचनमब्रुवन् ॥ १ ॥

paid their respect to him in due form and addressed him these words—(1).
#### 1.2
भगवन् सर्ववर्णानां यथावदनुपूर्वशः ।
अन्तरप्रभवानां च धर्मान्नो वक्तुमर्हसि ॥ २ ॥

May Thou, O blessed One, explain to us, in due form and in proper order, the duties of all castes and intermediate castes!—(2).
#### 1.5
आसीदिदं तमोभूतमप्रज्ञातमलक्षणम् ।

This (World) was in existence in the form, as it were, of dense Darkness,—unperceived, undifferentiated, 

अप्रतर्क्यमविज्ञेयं प्रसुप्तमिव सर्वतः ॥ ५ ॥

incogitable, (hence) incognizable; as it was wholly merged in deep sleep.—(5).
#### 1.6
ततः स्वयम्भूर्भगवानव्यक्तो व्यञ्जयन्निदम् ।

Thereafter, the supreme being (svayambhu) Hiraṇyagarbha, self-born, unmanifest and bringing into view this (universe), 

महाभूतादि वृत्तोजाः प्रादुरासीत् तमोनुदः ॥ ६ ॥

appeared,— dispelling darkness and having his (creative) power operating upon the Elemental Substances and other things.—(6)
#### 1.7
योऽसावतीन्द्रियग्राह्यः सूक्ष्मोऽव्यक्तः सनातनः ।

He,—who is apprehended beyond the senses, who is subtile, unmanifest and eternal, 

सर्वभूतमयोऽचिन्त्यः स एव स्वयमुद्बभौ ॥ ७ ॥

absorbed in all created things and inconceivable,—appeared by himself. (7)
#### 1.8
सोऽभिध्याय शरीरात् स्वात् सिसृक्षुर्विविधाः प्रजाः ।

Desiring to create the several kinds of created things, he, by mere willing, out of his own body, 

अप एव ससर्जादौ तासु वीर्यमवासृजत् ॥ ८ ॥

produced Water in the beginning; and in that he threw the seed.—(8)
#### 1.9
तदण्डमभवद्धैमं सहस्रांशुसमप्रभम् ।

That became the golden egg, resplendent like the Sun; 

तस्मिञ्जज्ञे स्वयं ब्रह्मा सर्वलोकपितामहः ॥ ९ ॥

in that (egg) he (Hiraṇyagarbha) himself was born as Brahmā, the ‘Grand-father’ of the whole world.—(9)
#### 1.10
आपो नारा इति प्रोक्ता आपो वै नरसूनवः ।

Water is called ‘nara,’—water being the offspring of nara; 

ता यदस्यायनं पूर्वं तेन नारायणः स्मृतः ॥ १० ॥

since water was the first thing created by (or, the original residence of) that being, he is, on that account, described as ‘nārāyaṇa.’—(10)
#### 1.11
यत् तत् कारणमव्यक्तं नित्यं सदसदात्मकम् ।

That which is the cause—unmanifest, eternal and partaking of the nature of the existent and the non-existent,—

तद्विसृष्टः स पुरुषो लोके ब्रह्मैति कीर्त्यते ॥ ११ ॥

the being produced by that (cause) is described among people as ‘brahmā.’—(11)
#### 1.12
तस्मिन्नण्डे स भगवानुषित्वा परिवत्सरम् ।

That supreme lord, having dwelt in that egg for a year, 

स्वयमेवात्मनो ध्यानात् तदण्डमकरोद् द्विधा ॥ १२ ॥

himself, by his own thought, broke that egg into two parts.—(12)
#### 1.13
ताभ्यां स शकलाभ्यां च दिवं भूमिं च निर्ममे ।

Out of those two pieces (of the egg) he formed Heaven and Earth, 

मध्ये व्योम दिशश्चाष्टावपां स्थानं च शाश्वतम् ॥ १३ ॥

and, between them, the (vyoma) Ākāśa, the eight quarters and the eternal receptacle of water.—(13)
#### 1.14
उद्बबर्हात्मनश्चैव मनः सदसदात्मकम् ।

From out of himself he brought forth the mind, which partakes of the nature of the existent and non-existent; 

मनसश्चाप्यहङ्कारमभिमन्तारमीश्वरम् ॥ ब्छ्।स्छ् ॥

and before the mind, he brought up the all-powerful principle of egoism, whose function consists in self-consciousness.—(14)
#### 1.15
महान्तमेव चात्मानं सर्वाणि त्रिगुणानि च ।

Also the all-pervading ‘mahat’ (the ‘great’ principle of intelligence); as also all those things that consist of the three constituent attributes, 

विषयाणां ग्रहीतॄणि शनैः पञ्चैन्द्रियाणि च ॥ १५ ॥

and in due course, also the five organs of sensation which apprehend objects.—(15)
#### 1.16
तेषां त्ववयवान् सूक्ष्मान् षण्णामप्यमितौजसाम् ।

Having combined the subtile components of the said six principles of illimitable potency 

संनिवेश्यात्ममात्रासु सर्वभूतानि निर्ममे ॥ १६ ॥

with their own evolutes, he created even all beings.—(16)
#### 1.17
यन् मूर्त्यवयवाः सूक्ष्मास्तानीमान्याश्रयन्ति षट् ।

Because the six subtile components of the frame (of primordial matter) enter into (produce) these, 

तस्माच्छरीरमित्याहुस्तस्य मूर्तिं मनीषिणः ॥ १७ ॥

therefore the wise ones have described the frame of that (primordial matter) as ‘body.’—(17)
#### 1.18
तदाविशन्ति भूतानि महान्ति सह कर्मभिः ।

The great elemental substances, along with their functions, enter 

मनश्चावयवैः सूक्ष्मैः सर्वभूतकृदव्ययम् ॥ १८ ॥

into that which (on that account) is the generator of all things and imperishable, as also the mind, along with its subtile components, .—(18)
#### 1.21
सर्वेषां तु स नामानि कर्माणि च पृथक् पृथक् ।

He designated distinct names and functions for all things; 

वेदशब्देभ्य एवादौ पृथक् संस्थाश्च निर्ममे ॥ २१ ॥

and devised Laws, on the basis of the words of the veda, at the outset .—(21)
#### 1.22
कर्मात्मनां च देवानां सोऽसृजत् प्राणिनां प्रभुः ।

For the sake of living beings intent upon action, as also the host of Gods 

साध्यानां च गणं सूक्ष्मं यज्ञं चैव सनातनम् ॥ २२ ॥

he created the eternal sacrifice, and the subtile multitude of the lesser divinities, the Sādhyas.—(22)
#### 1.23
अग्निवायुरविभ्यस्तु त्रयं ब्रह्म सनातनम् ।

From out of (the three deities) Agni, Vāyu and Ravi, he extracted, the eternal Brahman, threefold, 

दुदोह यज्ञसिद्ध्यर्थं ऋच्।यजुस्।सामलक्षणम् ॥ २३ ॥

in the forms of ‘Ṛk,’ ‘Yajuṣ’ and ‘Sāman’, for the due fulfilment of sacrifices.—(23)
#### 1.26
कर्मणां च विवेकार्थं धर्माधर्मौ व्यवेचयत् ।

For the due discrimination of actions, he differentiated Virtue and Vice; 

द्वन्द्वैरयोजयच्चैमाः सुखदुःखादिभिः प्रजाः ॥ २६ ॥

and he connected these creatures with such pairs of opposites as Pleasure-Pain and the like.—(26)
#### 1.28
यं तु कर्मणि यस्मिन् स न्ययुङ्क्त प्रथमं प्रभुः ।

The act to which the lord had, at first, directed him, 

स तदेव स्वयं भेजे सृज्यमानः पुनः पुनः ॥ २८ ॥

to that same, each being, naturally conformed when created again and again.—(28)
#### 1.29
हिंस्राहिंस्रे मृदुक्रूरे धर्माधर्मावृतानृते ।

Hurtfulness or harmlessness, tenderness or hard-heartedness, virtue or vice, truthfulness or truth-lessness,—

यद् यस्य सो'दधात् सर्गे तत् तस्य स्वयमाविशत् ॥ २९ ॥

each of these accrued to that being in which he implanted it at creation.—(29)
#### 1.30
यथर्तुलिङ्गान्यर्तवः स्वयमेवर्तुपर्यये ।

Just as on the approach of the turn of the Seasons, each season, by itself, acquires its own seasonal characteristics,—

स्वानि स्वान्यभिपद्यन्ते तथा कर्माणि देहिनः ॥ ३० ॥

so in the same manner living beings take up their respective lines of action.—(30)
#### 1.31
लोकानां तु विवृद्ध्यर्थं मुखबाहूरुपादतः ।

With a view to the development of the (three) regions, , from out of His mouth, arms, thighs and feet (respectively), 

ब्राह्मणं क्षत्रियं वैश्यं शूद्रं च निरवर्तयत् ॥ ३१ ॥

He brought into existence the Brāhmaṇa, the Kṣatriya, the Vaiśya and the Śūdra.—(31)
#### 1.32
द्विधा कृत्वाऽत्मनो देहमर्धेन पुरुषोऽभवत् ।

Having divided his body into two halves, with the one half, the Lord became Male, 

अर्धेन नारी तस्यां स विराजमसृजत् प्रभुः ॥ ३२ ॥

and with the other half, Female; from her he produced Virāj.—(32)
#### 1.33
तपस्तप्त्वाऽसृजद् यं तु स स्वयं पुरुषो विराट् ।

That whom the said Being Virāj himself, after having performed austerities, produced, 

तं मां वित्तास्य सर्वस्य स्रष्टारं द्विजसत्तमाः ॥ ३३ ॥

know me to be the same, the creator, of this whole (would), O best of Brāhmaṇas.—(33)
#### 1.34
अहं प्रजाः सिसृक्षुस्तु तपस्तप्त्वा सुदुश्चरम् ।

Being desirous of bringing into existence the (various kinds of) created beings, I, at the very outset, performed most arduous austerities 

पतीन् प्रजानामसृजं महर्षीनादितो दश ॥ ३४ ॥

and called into being the ten great sages, the directors of all created things; (34)—viz: 
#### 1.35
मरीचिमत्र्यङ्गिरसौ पुलस्त्यं पुलहं क्रतुम् ।

Marīci, Atri, Aṅgiras, Pulastya, Pulaha, Kratu, 

प्रचेतसं वसिष्ठं च भृगुं नारदमेव च ॥ ३५ ॥

Pracetas, Vaśiṣṭha, Bhṛgu and also Nārada.—(35)
#### 1.51
एवं सर्वं स सृष्ट्वैदं मां चाचिन्त्यपराक्रमः ।

He, of inconceivable power, created all this and also myself; [he directed me to maintain it] and 

आत्मन्यन्तर्दधे भूयः कालं कालेन पीडयन् ॥ ५१ ॥

Thus repeatedly suppressing time (of dissolution) by time (of creation and maintenance), disappeared within himself.—(51)
#### 1.52
यदा स देवो जागर्ति तदेवं चेष्टते जगत् ।

When that divine being is awake, then this world is active; 

यदा स्वपिति शान्तात्मा तदा सर्वं निमीलति ॥ ५२ ॥

when he slumbers, with his mind in calm repose, then all vanishes.—(52)
#### 1.53
तस्मिन् स्वपिति तु स्वस्थे कर्मात्मानः शरीरिणः ।

When he slumbers, having retired within himself, all active embodied beings 

स्वकर्मभ्यो निवर्तन्ते मनश्च ग्लानिमृच्छति ॥ ५३ ॥

desist from their actions, and their mind falls into depression.—(53)
#### 1.54
युगपत् तु प्रलीयन्ते यदा तस्मिन् महात्मनि ।

When all things become absorbed all at once in that Great Soul, 

तदाऽयं सर्वभूतात्मा सुखं स्वपिति निर्वृतः ॥ ५४ ॥

then this Soul of all things sleeps happy and contented .—(54)
#### 1.58
इदं शास्त्रं तु कृत्वाऽसौ मामेव स्वयमादितः ।

Having propounded this Law, he himself, first of all, taught it to me 

विधिवद् ग्राहयामास मरीच्यादींस्त्वहं मुनीन् ॥ ५८ ॥

with due care; I then taught it to Marīci and other Sages.—(58)

#### 1.59
एतद् वोऽयं भृगुः शास्त्रं श्रावयिष्यत्यशेषतः ।

This Bhṛgu will fully describe to you this (Law); 

एतद् हि मत्तोऽधिजगे सर्वमेषोऽखिलं मुनिः ॥ ५९ ॥

this sage has learnt the whole of this in its entirety, from me.—(59)

#### 6.1
एवं गृहाश्रमे स्थित्वा विधिवत् स्नातको द्विजः ।
वने वसेत् तु नियतो यथावद् विजितैन्द्रियः 

The twice-born accomplished student, having, in the afore- said manner, lived, according to law, the life of the householder, should dwell in the forest, in the proper manner, self-controlled and with his organs under subjection—(1).
#### 6.2
गृहस्थस्तु यथा पश्येद् वलीपलितमात्मनः ।
अपत्यस्यैव चापत्यं तदाऽरण्यं समाश्रयेत् ॥ २ ॥

When the householder notices his wrinkles and greyness, and sees his child’s child,—then he should r etire to the forest,—(2).
#### 6.5
मुन्यन्नैर्विविधैर्मेध्यैः शाकमूलफलेन वा ।
एतानेव महायज्ञान्निर्वपेद् विधिपूर्वकम् ॥ ५ ॥

These same ‘Great Sacrifices’ he should offer, according to rule, with various kinds of pure food fit for hermits, or with herbs, roots and fruits.
#### 6.21
पुष्पमूलफलैर्वाऽपि केवलैर्वर्तयेत् सदा ।
कालपक्वैः स्वयं शीर्णैर्वैखानसमते स्थितः ॥ २१ ॥

Or, he may always subsist only on flowers, roots and fruits, which have ripened in their own season and fallen down spontaneously,—keeping firm in the ways of life prescribed in the ‘Vaikhānasa’ Institutes.—(21).
#### 6.22
भूमौ विपरिवर्तेत तिष्ठेद् वा प्रपदैर्दिनम् ।
स्थानासनाभ्यां विहरेत् सवनेषूपयन्नपः ॥ २२ ॥

He shall roll about on the ground, or stand on tip-toe during the day; he shall beguile his time by standing and sitting, going to water at the ‘Savanas’.—(22).
#### 6.31
अपराजितां वाऽस्थाय व्रजेद् दिशमजिह्मगः ।
आ निपातात्शरीरस्य युक्तो वार्यनिलाशनः ॥ ३१ ॥

Or, having fixed upon the North-Easterly direction, he shall go forward, moving straight on, intent and living upon water and air,—till the falling off of his body.—(31).
#### 6.33
वनेषु च विहृत्यैवं तृतीयं भागमायुषः ।
चतुर्थमायुषो भागं त्यक्वा सङ्गान् परिव्रजेत् ॥ ३३ ॥

Having thus passed the third part of his life in the forest, the man shall, during the fourth part, renounce all attachments and go forth (a wandering mendicant).
#### 6.32
आसां महर्षिचर्याणां त्यक्त्वाऽन्यतमया तनुम् ।
वीतशोकभयो विप्रो ब्रह्मलोके महीयते ॥ ३२ ॥

Having discarded his body by one of these methods adopted by the Great Sages, the Brāhmaṇa, with sorrow and fear departed, becomes exalted in the region of Brahman.—(32).
#### 6.87
ब्रह्मचारी गृहस्थश्च वानप्रस्थो यतिस्तथा ।
एते गृहस्थप्रभवाश्चत्वारः पृथगाश्रमाः ॥ ८७ ॥

The student, the Householder, the Hermit and the Renunciate,—all these, several stages emanate from the Householder.—(87). 
#### 6.88
सर्वेऽपि क्रमशस्त्वेते यथाशास्त्रं निषेविताः ।
यथोक्तकारिणं विप्रं नयन्ति परमां गतिम् ॥ ८८ ॥

But all these, when observed in due order, a ccording to the scriptures lead the Brāhmaṇa who has (thus) acted according to the law, to the highest state.—(88).

### 10.45
मुखबाहूरुपद्जानां या लोके जातयो बहिः ।
म्लेच्छवाचश्चार्यवाचः सर्वे ते दस्यवः स्मृताः ॥ ४५ ॥

All those races of the world which are outside the pale of the people ‘born of the mouth, the arms, the thighs and the feet,’—speaking the (mleccha) ‘barbaric’ or the (ārya) ‘refined’ language—are called ‘Dasyu.’—(45)
