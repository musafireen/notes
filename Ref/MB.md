शरभः शलभश्चैव सूर्याचन्द्रमसौ तथा |

दिदृक्षुर्जप्यपरमः सर्वकृच्छ्रधरः स्थितः ||२||

Desiring to see the supreme one, he remained there, suffering all the hardships.

The brahmana and great sage was single-minded and remained there, with his arms upraised. 

स्तोत्रं जगौ स विश्वाय निर्गुणाय महात्मने ||३||

He chanted this stotra1129 to the great-souled one, who is without qualities and is the universe.”

There were one thousand stomachs and one thousand arms and some of those could not be seen.

ओङ्कारमुद्गिरन्वक्त्रात्सावित्रीं च तदन्वयाम् ||७||

With one of his mouths, he chanted ‘Om’. With another mouth, he chanted ‘Savitri’.

When this was over, with other mouths, he chanted the beneficial four Vedas.

The Aranayakas were also under the god, Hari Narayana’s, control.

In his hands, he held a sacrificial altar, a kamandalu, some darbha grass, some gems, a pair of sandals, a deerskin, a wooden staff and a blazing fire. 

धारयामास देवेशो हस्तैर्यज्ञपतिस्तदा ||९||

The lord of gods and the lord of sacrifices held these in his hands and was cheerful. 
###### 10
तं प्रसन्नं प्रसन्नात्मा नारदो द्विजसत्तमः |

Narada, supreme among brahmanas, was delighted at this.

वाग्यतः प्रयतो भूत्वा ववन्दे परमेश्वरम् ||१०||

Restrained in speech, he bowed down before the supreme lord.

तमुवाच नतं मूर्ध्ना देवानामादिरव्ययः ||१०||

While his head was still bowed down, the first and undecaying god said
###### 11

इमं देशमनुप्राप्ता मम दर्शनलालसाः ||११||

###### 12

However, they could not see me. 

न च द्रक्ष्यति कश्चन |

With the exception of someone who is single-minded in devotion towards me, no one can see me. O brahmana! It is my view that you are thus single-minded.
###### 17

###### 18
इमे ह्यनिन्द्रियाहारा मद्भक्ताश्चन्द्रवर्चसः |

These devotees of mine are like the moon in their complexions. They are devoid of senses and do not eat. 

एकाग्राश्चिन्तयेयुर्मां 

They single-mindedly think of me. 

नैषां विघ्नो भवेदिति ||१८||

Let there not be any obstructions in their pursuits. 
###### 19
सिद्धाश्चैते महाभागाः 

They are successful and immensely fortunate. 

पुरा ह्येकान्तिनोऽभवन् |

In ancient times, they were single-mindedly devoted to me. 

तमोरजोविनिर्मुक्ता 

They have been freed from tamas and rajas. 

मां प्रवेक्ष्यन्त्यसंशयम् ||१९||

There is no doubt that they will merge into me.
###### 23
द्विर्द्वादशेभ्यस्तत्त्वेभ्यः ख्यातो यः पञ्चविंशकः |

He is beyond the twenty-four principles and is known as the twenty-fifth. 

पुरुषो निष्क्रियश्चैव ज्ञानदृश्यश्च कथ्यते ||२३||

He is the passive Purusha and is said to be comprehended through knowledge.
###### 24
यं प्रविश्य भवन्तीह मुक्ता वै द्विजसत्तम |

O supreme among brahmanas! A person who merges into him is emancipated. 

स वासुदेवो विज्ञेयः परमात्मा सनातनः ||२४||

He is known as Vasudeva. He is the eternal paramatman.
###### 28
जगत्प्रतिष्ठा देवर्षे पृथिव्यप्सु प्रलीयते |

O devarshi! When the universe is destroyed, the earth merges into water. 

ज्योतिष्यापः प्रलीयन्ते 

Water merges into light. 

ज्योतिर्वायौ प्रलीयते ||२८||

Light merges into the wind. 
###### 29
खे वायुः प्रलयं याति 

The wind merges into space. 

मनस्याकाशमेव च |

Space merges into the mind. 

मनो हि परमं भूतं तदव्यक्ते प्रलीयते ||२९||

The mind is a supreme element and it merges into the unmanifest.1169 
###### 30
अव्यक्तं पुरुषे ब्रह्मन्निष्क्रिये सम्प्रलीयते |

O brahmana! The unmanifest merges into the inactive Purusha. 

नास्ति तस्मात्परतरं पुरुषाद्वै सनातनात् ||३०||

There is nothing that is superior to the eternal Purusha. 
###### 31
नित्यं हि नास्ति जगति भूतं स्थावरजङ्गमम् |

There is no creation in the universe, mobile or immobile, that is eternal. 

ऋते तमेकं पुरुषं वासुदेवं सनातनम् ||३१||

The only single exception is the eternal Purusha Vasudeva.

सर्वभूतात्मभूतो हि वासुदेवो महाबलः ||३१||

The immensely powerful Vasudeva is in the atmans of all creatures.
###### 34
न विना धातुसङ्घातं शरीरं भवति क्वचित् |

Without a combination of the elements, there cannot be a body. 

न च जीवं विना ब्रह्मन्धातवश्चेष्टयन्त्युत ||३४||

O brahmana! Without the jivatman, that combination of elements wouldn’t have moved. 
###### 35
स जीवः परिसङ्ख्यातः शेषः सङ्कर्षणः प्रभुः |

That jivatman is also known as the lord Shesha or Samkarshana. 

तस्मात्सनत्कुमारत्वं यो लभेत स्वकर्मणा ||३५||

Using his own deeds, the one who arises from this is known as Sanatkumara. 
###### 36
यस्मिंश्च सर्वभूतानि प्रलयं यान्ति सङ्क्षये |

When all creatures are destroyed, it is into him that they merge. 

स मनः सर्वभूतानां प्रद्युम्नः परिपठ्यते ||३६||

The mind of all creatures is known as Pradyumna. 
###### 37
तस्मात्प्रसूतो यः कर्ता कार्यं कारणमेव च |

It is from him1170 that the the doer and cause and effect arise. 

यस्मात्सर्वं प्रभवति जगत्स्थावरजङ्गमम् ||३७||
सोऽनिरुद्धः

Everything in the universe, mobile and immobile, is generated from Aniruddha. 

स ईशानो व्यक्तिः सा सर्वकर्मसु ||३७||

He is also known as Ishana and he manifests himself through all his deeds. 
###### 38
यो वासुदेवो भगवान्क्षेत्रज्ञो निर्गुणात्मकः |

The illustrious Vasudeva is kshetrajna and possesses no qualities in his soul. 

ज्ञेयः स एव भगवाञ्जीवः सङ्कर्षणः प्रभुः ||३८||

When that illustrious one is born in any creature, he is known as the lord Samkarshana. 
###### 39
सङ्कर्षणाच्च प्रद्युम्नो मनोभूतः स उच्यते |

Pradyumna is said to be generated from the mind and is born from Samkarshana. 

प्रद्युम्नाद्योऽनिरुद्धस्तु सोऽहङ्कारो महेश्वरः ||३९||

Aniruddha is generated from Pradyumna and is consciousness, or Maheshvara.
###### 43
माया ह्येषा मया सृष्टा यन्मां पश्यसि नारद |

Narada! That you can see me is only because of a maya that has been created by me. 

सर्वभूतगुणैर्युक्तं नैवं त्वं ज्ञातुमर्हसि ||४३||

I seem to possess the qualities of all creatures, but that’s because you cannot comprehend me.
###### 47
हिरण्यगर्भो लोकादिश्चतुर्वक्त्रो निरुक्तगः |

I am Hiranyagarbha, the origin of the worlds. I have four faces and can be understood through nirukta. 

ब्रह्मा सनातनो देवो मम बह्वर्थचिन्तकः ||४७||

The eternal god Brahma thinks of me in many ways. 
###### 48
पश्यैकादश मे रुद्रान्दक्षिणं पार्श्वमास्थितान् |

Behold! The eleven Rudras are established on my right. 

द्वादशैव तथादित्यान्वामं पार्श्वं समास्थितान् ||४८||

The twelve Adityas are established on my left. 
###### 49
अग्रतश्चैव मे पश्य वसूनष्टौ सुरोत्तमान् |

Behold! The eight Vasus, supreme among gods, are established to my front. 

नासत्यं चैव दस्रं च भिषजौ पश्य पृष्ठतः ||४९||

Behold! Nasatya and Dasra, the physicians,1171 are to my rear.
###### 56
अहं हयशिरो भूत्वा समुद्रे पश्चिमोत्तरे |

Along the northern and the western ocean, I became Hayashira. 

पिबामि सुहुतं हव्यं कव्यं च श्रद्धयान्वितम् ||५६||

I drank the havya and the kavya, offered faithfully, to the accompaniment of mantras.
###### 61
प्रादुर्भावगतश्चाहं सुरकार्येषु नित्यदा |

To accomplish the tasks of the gods, I will always manifest myself. 

अनुशास्यस्त्वया ब्रह्मन्नियोज्यश्च सुतो यथा ||६१||

O Brahma! For such purposes, you can always instruct me, like a father instructs his son.”
###### 62
एतांश्चान्यांश्च रुचिरान्ब्रह्मणेऽमिततेजसे |

अहं दत्त्वा वरान्प्रीतो निवृत्तिपरमोऽभवम् ||६२||

Thereafter, I again resorted to nivritti. 
###### 63
निर्वाणं सर्वधर्माणां निवृत्तिः परमा स्मृता |

Among all the kinds of dharma that lead to emancipation, nivritti is said to be the supreme. 

तस्मान्निवृत्तिमापन्नश्चरेत्सर्वाङ्गनिर्वृतः ||६३||

That is the reason one should follow nivritti and act so as to withdraw all one’s limbs. 
###### 64
विद्यासहायवन्तं मामादित्यस्थं सनातनम् |
कपिलं प्राहुराचार्याः साङ्ख्यनिश्चितनिश्चयाः ||६४||

Preceptors like Kapila have set out their firm conclusions in the knowledge of sankhya. That is an aid in advancing towards my eternal self, with the resplendence of the sun. 
###### 65
हिरण्यगर्भो भगवानेष छन्दसि सुष्टुतः |

Metrical compositions have praised me as the illustrious Hiranyagarbha. 

सोऽहं योगगतिर्ब्रह्मन्योगशास्त्रेषु शब्दितः ||६५||

O brahmana! In the sacred texts of yoga, I have been spoken about as the objective of yoga. 
###### 66
एषोऽहं व्यक्तिमागम्य तिष्ठामि दिवि शाश्वतः |

I am established in the eternal firmament and am thus manifest in the sacred texts. 

ततो युगसहस्रान्ते संहरिष्ये जगत्पुनः ||६६||

At the end of one thousand yugas, I will destroy the universe again. 

कृत्वात्मस्थानि भूतानि स्थावराणि चराणि च ||६६||

I will withdraw all mobile and immobile objects into myself. 
###### 67
एकाकी विद्यया सार्धं विहरिष्ये द्विजोत्तम |

O supreme among brahmanas! I will then exist alone, with nothing except knowledge. 

ततो भूयो जगत्सर्वं करिष्यामीह विद्यया ||६७||

Using that knowledge, I will again create everything in the universe.
###### 69
प्रद्युम्नादनिरुद्धोऽहं सर्गो मम पुनः पुनः |

From Pradyumna, I repeatedly create myself as Aniruddha.

अनिरुद्धात्तथा ब्रह्मा तत्रादिकमलोद्भवः ||६९||

Brahma, the one who is born from the lotus, results from Aniruddha. 
###### 73
हिरण्याक्षं हनिष्यामि दैतेयं बलगर्वितम् |

"I will slay the daitya Hiranyaksha, intoxicated of his valour.1178 

नारसिंहं वपुः कृत्वा हिरण्यकशिपुं पुनः ||७३||
सुरकार्ये हनिष्यामि यज्ञघ्नं दितिनन्दनम् ||७३||

To accomplish the tasks of the gods, I again will slay Diti’s son, Hiranyakashipu, the destroyer of sacrifices, in the form of narasimha.1179 
###### 74
विरोचनस्य बलवान्बलिः पुत्रो महासुरः |

The great asura Bali will be the powerful Virochana’s son. 

भविष्यति स शक्रं च स्वराज्याच्च्यावयिष्यति ||७४||

He will dislodge Shakra from his own kingdom. 
###### 75
त्रैलोक्येऽपहृते तेन विमुखे च शचीपतौ |

Defeating Shachi’s consort, he will deprive him of the three worlds. 

अदित्यां द्वादशः पुत्रः सम्भविष्यामि कश्यपात् ||७५||

I will be born as the twelfth son of Aditi and Kashyapa.1180
###### 76
ततो राज्यं प्रदास्यामि शक्रायामिततेजसे |

I will give the kingdom back to the infinitely energetic Shakra. 

देवताः स्थापयिष्यामि स्वेषु स्थानेषु नारद ||७६||

O Narada! I will establish the gods in their own places. 

बलिं चैव करिष्यामि पातालतलवासिनम् ||७६||

I will act so that Bali becomes a resident of the nether regions. 
###### 77
त्रेतायुगे भविष्यामि रामो भृगुकुलोद्वहः |

In treta yuga, in the lineage of Bhrigu, I will be born as Rama.1181 

क्षत्रं चोत्सादयिष्यामि समृद्धबलवाहनम् ||७७||

I will exterminate the kshatriyas, powerful with their soldiers and mounts. 
###### 78
सन्धौ तु समनुप्राप्ते त्रेतायां द्वापरस्य च |
रामो दाशरथिर्भूत्वा भविष्यामि जगत्पतिः ||७८||

At the conjunction of treta and dvapara, I will be born as King Rama, the son of Dasharatha" (।)
###### 90
समागतेषु बलिषु पृथिव्यां सर्वराजसु |
वासविः सुसहायो वै मम ह्येको भविष्यति ||९०||

When the armies of all the kings on earth come together, I alone will be the excellent aide to Vasava’s son.1188 
###### 91
एवं लोका वदिष्यन्ति नरनारायणावृषी |

The worlds will speak of us as the rishis Nara and Narayana. 

उद्युक्तौ दहतः क्षत्रं लोककार्यार्थमीश्वरौ ||९१||

To accomplish the objectives of the world, these two lords will consume the kshatriyas. 
###### 92
कृत्वा भारावतरणं वसुधाया यथेप्सितम् |

As is desired, we will reduce the burden of the world. 

सर्वसात्वतमुख्यानां द्वारकायाश्च सत्तम ||९२||
करिष्ये प्रलयं घोरमात्मज्ञातिविनाशनम् ||९२||

O excellent one! I will create a terrible destruction that will devastate my own kin and absorb the foremost among the Satvatas and Dvaraka into my own self. 
###### 99
नारदोऽपि महातेजाः प्राप्यानुग्रहमीप्सितम् |

The extremely energetic Narada obtained the desired favour. 

नरनारायणौ द्रष्टुं प्राद्रवद्बदराश्रमम् ||९९||

He went and saw Nara and Narayana in the hermitage of Badari. 
###### 100
इदं महोपनिषदं चतुर्वेदसमन्वितम् |
साङ्ख्ययोगकृतं तेन पञ्चरात्रानुशब्दितम् ||१००||

This great Upanishad1190 is in conformity with the four Vedas and sankhya and yoga, and is referred to by this name in the pancharatra rituals.
###### 105
सर्गस्यादौ स्मृतो ब्रह्मा प्रजासर्गकरः प्रभुः |

At the beginning of every cycle of creation, Brahma is thought of as the lord who creates creatures. 

जानाति देवप्रवरं भूयश्चातोऽधिकं नृप ||१०५||

O king! He knows that the supreme among gods1191 is superior to him. 

परमात्मानमीशानमात्मनः प्रभवं तथा ||१०५||

He is the paramatman. He is the powerful lord of the atmans. 
###### 106
ये त्वन्ये ब्रह्मसदने सिद्धसङ्घाः समागताः |
तेभ्यस्तच्छ्रावयामास पुराणं वेदसंमितम् ||१०६||

There were large numbers of siddhas who assembled in Brahma’s abode and they wished to hear about the ancient accounts that were in conformity with the Vedas. 
###### 107
तेषां सकाशात्सूर्यश्च श्रुत्वा वै भावितात्मनाम् |
आत्मानुगामिनां ब्रह्म श्रावयामास भारत ||१०७||

He recited it to them.1192 There, Surya heard it from the brahmana with the cleansed soul. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! 
###### 108
षट्षष्टिर्हि सहस्राणि ऋषीणां भावितात्मनाम् |

He repeated it to the sixty-six thousand rishis, with cleansed souls, who follow him.1193 

सूर्यस्य तपतो लोकान्निर्मिता ये पुरःसराः ||१०८||

Surya had earlier been created to heat the worlds. 

तेषामकथयत्सूर्यः सर्वेषां भावितात्मनाम् ||१०८||

Surya repeated the account to all those with cleansed souls. 
###### 109
सूर्यानुगामिभिस्तात ऋषिभिस्तैर्महात्मभिः |

O son! Those great-souled rishis were Surya’s followers. 

मेरौ समागता देवाः श्राविताश्चेदमुत्तमम् ||१०९||

They repeated this excellent account to the gods who assembled on Meru. 
###### 110

स्वमांसं परमांसेन यो वर्धयितुमिच्छति |

If a person seeks to enhance his own flesh by eating the flesh of others, 

उद्विग्नवासे वसति यत्रतत्राभिजायते ||३४||

he always dwells in anxiety and is reborn as inferior species.
###### 13.116.36
इदं तु खलु कौन्तेय श्रुतमासीत्पुरा मया |

O Kounteya! I heard everything about this earlier.

मार्कण्डेयस्य वदतो ये दोषा मांसभक्षणे ||३६||

Markandeya spoke about the sins associated with eating meat. 
###### 13.116.37
यो हि खादति मांसानि प्राणिनां जीवितार्थिनाम् |
हतानां वा मृतानां वा यथा हन्ता तथैव सः ||३७||

If a person eats the flesh of animals that seek to remain alive, whether he slays them or whether they are already dead, it is as if he himself is the slayer.
###### 13.116.38
धनेन क्रायको हन्ति खादकश्चोपभोगतः |
घातको वधबन्धाभ्यामित्येष त्रिविधो वधः ||३८||

There are three kinds of slaughter—killing by using wealth to purchase, killing by eating and directly killing by binding and slaying.
###### 13.116.42
अप्रोक्षितं वृथामांसं विधिहीनं न भक्षयेत् |

“One should never eat futile meat that has not been offered in sacrifices, without following the rites. 

भक्षयन्निरयं याति नरो नास्त्यत्र संशयः ||४२||

There is no doubt that a man who eats this will go to (niraya) hell.
###### 13.116.43
प्रोक्षिताभ्युक्षितं मांसं तथा ब्राह्मणकाम्यया |
अल्पदोषमिह ज्ञेयं 

Know that if one eats meat that has been offered at sacrifices or has been killed for the sake of brahmanas, there is a little bit of sin.

विपरीते तु लिप्यते ||४३||

However, by acting in a contrary way, one is touched by sin. 
###### 13.116.44
खादकस्य कृते जन्तुं यो हन्यात्पुरुषाधमः |

A person who kills animals for someone else to eat is the worst among men.

महादोषकरस्तत्र खादको न तु घातकः ||४४||

But the greater sin is attached to the eater, not to the killer.
###### 13.116.45
इज्यायज्ञश्रुतिकृतैर्यो मार्गैरबुधो जनः |
हन्याज्जन्तुं मांसगृद्ध्री स वै नरकभाङ्नरः ||४५||

If an ignorant person wishes to follow the path of the rites and sacrifices laid down in the sacred texts, but nevertheless kills animals in a desire for their flesh, such a man goes to hell.
